I am currently earning my MA in preparation for PhD study in Philosophy. My primary research interests are in metaphysics and philosophy of science but I'm happy to discuss topics in metaethics, epistemology and philosophy of language as well. This website is currently under construction but will serve as a centralized source for my works in progress, all future publications and any other relevant academic.

As of late, I've been working on a paper related to the ongoing metaontological debate between Ted Sider and Eli Hirsch concerning quantifier variance. If you're interested in reading it, I'm happy for all comments, questions and critiques.

Papers in Progress

  1. Interpretation Magnetism
    I extend a recent treatment Jonathan Schaffer has given for the ongoing metaontological debate between Eli Hirsch and Ted Sider on quantifier variance. I argue for my eponymous solution to Schaffer's "Stability Problem" that privileges the interpretation of an existence predicate across all domains.
